# US Airports Reference

## Salt Lake City International Airport
- IATA: `SLC` | ICAO: `KSLC`
- Location: Salt Lake City, Utah
- Terminals: 1 (single modern terminal opened 2020)
- Main airlines: Delta (hub), Southwest, United, American
- Interesting fact: The airport sits at 4,227 feet elevation — one of the highest major US airports — which affects aircraft performance calculations

## John F. Kennedy International Airport
- IATA: `JFK` | ICAO: `KJFK`
- Location: Queens, New York City
- Terminals: 6 active terminals (T1, T2, T4, T5, T7, T8)
- Main airlines: Delta, American, JetBlue (focus city), United
- Interesting fact: JFK handles more international passengers than any other US airport

## Los Angeles International Airport
- IATA: `LAX` | ICAO: `KLAX`
- Location: Los Angeles, California
- Terminals: 9 terminals including the Tom Bradley International Terminal
- Main airlines: United, Delta, American, Southwest, Alaska
- Interesting fact: LAX has two parallel pairs of runways, allowing simultaneous operations on four runways

## Chicago O'Hare International Airport
- IATA: `ORD` | ICAO: `KORD`
- Location: Chicago, Illinois
- Terminals: 4 terminals (Terminals 1, 2, 3, and International Terminal 5)
- Main airlines: United (hub), American (hub)
- Interesting fact: The ICAO code KORD comes from Orchard Field, the airport's original name

## Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport
- IATA: `ATL` | ICAO: `KATL`
- Location: Atlanta, Georgia
- Terminals: 2 terminals (Domestic and International) connected by an underground train
- Main airlines: Delta (superHub — largest Delta operation worldwide)
- Interesting fact: ATL is consistently one of the busiest airports in the world by passenger count

## Dallas Fort Worth International Airport
- IATA: `DFW` | ICAO: `KDFW`
- Location: Between Dallas and Fort Worth, Texas
- Terminals: 5 terminals (A, B, C, D, E)
- Main airlines: American (hub)
- Interesting fact: DFW is larger in land area than Manhattan

## Denver International Airport
- IATA: `DEN` | ICAO: `KDEN`
- Location: Denver, Colorado
- Terminals: 1 main terminal (Jeppesen) with 3 concourses
- Main airlines: United (hub), Southwest, Frontier (hub)
- Interesting fact: DEN sits at 5,431 feet elevation and its distinctive white tent roof is designed to look like the Rocky Mountains

## Seattle-Tacoma International Airport
- IATA: `SEA` | ICAO: `KSEA`
- Location: SeaTac, Washington
- Terminals: 1 main terminal with North and South satellite concourses
- Main airlines: Alaska (hub), Delta, Southwest, United
- Interesting fact: SEA is a major gateway to Asia-Pacific destinations

## Miami International Airport
- IATA: `MIA` | ICAO: `KMIA`
- Location: Miami, Florida
- Terminals: 1 terminal with North and South terminals
- Main airlines: American (hub — largest American operation), LATAM, Avianca
- Interesting fact: MIA handles more international freight than any other US airport

## Boston Logan International Airport
- IATA: `BOS` | ICAO: `KBOS`
- Location: Boston, Massachusetts
- Terminals: 4 terminals (A, B, C, E)
- Main airlines: JetBlue (focus city), Delta, American, United
- Interesting fact: Logan is one of the few major US airports located directly in a city, just 3 miles from downtown Boston

## San Francisco International Airport
- IATA: `SFO` | ICAO: `KSFO`
- Location: San Mateo County, California (14 miles south of San Francisco)
- Terminals: 4 terminals with an international terminal
- Main airlines: United (hub), Delta, Alaska, American
- Interesting fact: SFO sits on reclaimed bay land and is subject to fog delays, especially in summer

## Harry Reid International Airport (Las Vegas)
- IATA: `LAS` | ICAO: `KLAS`
- Location: Las Vegas, Nevada
- Terminals: 2 terminals (T1 and T3)
- Main airlines: Southwest, Delta, United, American, Spirit, Allegiant
- Interesting fact: LAS is one of the busiest leisure travel airports in the US with few connecting passengers — most travelers are origin or destination

## Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport
- IATA: `PHX` | ICAO: `KPHX`
- Location: Phoenix, Arizona
- Terminals: 3 terminals (T2, T3, T4)
- Main airlines: American (hub), Southwest, Delta, United
- Interesting fact: PHX is one of the few major US airports located within city limits, just 3 miles from downtown Phoenix

## Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport
- IATA: `MSP` | ICAO: `KMSP`
- Location: Minneapolis, Minnesota
- Terminals: 2 terminals (Lindbergh and Humphrey)
- Main airlines: Delta (hub), Sun Country (hub), United, American
- Interesting fact: MSP is consistently rated among the top large airports in the US for customer satisfaction

## Portland International Airport
- IATA: `PDX` | ICAO: `KPDX`
- Location: Portland, Oregon
- Terminals: 1 terminal with multiple concourses
- Main airlines: Alaska, Delta, Southwest, United
- Interesting fact: PDX is famous for its carpet pattern, which became a regional cultural icon and is sold as merchandise
